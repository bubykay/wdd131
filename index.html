Ekiti, Nigeria

Ekiti State, located in the southwestern region of Nigeria, is known for its rich cultural heritage, lush landscapes, and a strong emphasis on education. Established on October 1, 1996, the state has its capital in Ado-Ekiti and comprises 16 local government areas. Ekiti is often referred to as the "Fountain of Knowledge" due to its impressive number of educational institutions and a history of producing distinguished academics and professionals. The state is home to notable landmarks like the Ikogosi Warm Springs, where warm and cold springs naturally meet, and Erin Ayonigba Sacred Fish River in Ero.

Agriculture is a significant part of Ekiti's economy, with crops like cocoa, yam, and cassava commonly cultivated. The state also prides itself on its vibrant festivals and traditions, such as the annual Ekiti State Cultural Festival, which showcases the diverse heritage of its people. With its serene environment and a reputation for hospitality, Ekiti State is both a cultural hub and a destination for eco-tourism and historical exploration.
